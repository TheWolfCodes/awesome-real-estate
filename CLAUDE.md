# CLAUDE.md

## Writing register — HARD RULE (all human-facing copy)

Applies to everything drafted for a human to read: emails, website/app/marketing
copy, UI strings, support replies, outreach messages, social posts.

- **Plain, everyday language.** Use the simple, common words a normal person actually
  uses. No literary or "elevated" vocabulary to sound smart or to save space.
- **No AI voice.** Drop the formulaic symmetry ("it's not about X, it's about Y"),
  greeting-card phrasing, and over-balanced tricolons — they read as machine-written.
- **Don't lecture.** Say the thing and stop. No unrequested explanation, no recap of
  what the reader already knows, no moralizing.
- **Match the reader's real level** — especially non-native English speakers: short
  sentences, concrete words, nothing they'd need a dictionary for.
- **Gut check before shipping:** read it back. Would a real person actually send this?
  If it sounds like a brochure or an essay, rewrite it simpler.
